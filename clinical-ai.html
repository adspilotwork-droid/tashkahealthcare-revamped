In-Depth FAQ

Everything Hospital Leaders Ask About Clinical Ai Chatbot Hospital India

Detailed answers written for hospital decision-makers — not product brochures. If you're evaluating clinical AI chatbot hospital India for your hospital, these questions cover what actually matters.

How is Tashka's Clinical AI different from using ChatGPT or Gemini in a hospital?

ChatGPT and Gemini are general-purpose language models trained on public internet data. They have no knowledge of your specific patients, your hospital's SOPs, or your billing system. Tashka's Clinical AI uses RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) — it generates answers by searching your hospital's own data sources in real time. It knows that Patient Ramesh Kumar in Ward 3 has pending lab results because it retrieved that from your LIS. ChatGPT cannot do this. Additionally, every Tashka AI response is audit-logged and role-gated — nurses see different information than doctors — which general-purpose AI tools cannot provide. Most importantly, your patient data never leaves your infrastructure to train external models.

What data sources does Tashka Clinical AI access?

Tashka's Clinical AI connects to: EMR/HIS patient records, LIS (lab results and pending tests), Pharmacy dispensing records, PACS/RIS radiology reports (where integrated), Billing and RCM data (for billing status queries), Clinical documents (discharge summaries, OT notes, nursing notes), SOP repositories and knowledge bases, and Administrative data (bed occupancy, census, pending discharges). All data access is permission-controlled and logged. The AI only accesses data that the logged-in user's role is permitted to see — a billing clerk cannot retrieve clinical notes through the AI that they couldn't access directly in the system.

Is Tashka's Clinical AI compliant with India's DPDPA (Digital Personal Data Protection Act)?

Yes. Tashka's Clinical AI is designed to be compliant with India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 by architecture: Data minimisation — the AI only retrieves data necessary to answer the specific query. Purpose limitation — patient data is used only for clinical care queries within the hospital, not for AI training or commercial analytics. Data localization — patient data remains on your hospital's infrastructure and is not sent to external cloud AI services for processing. Audit trails — every data access is logged with timestamp, user identity, and query content, enabling compliance reporting. Hospitals with specific DPDPA compliance requirements should discuss their needs with Tashka's implementation team during Discovery.
