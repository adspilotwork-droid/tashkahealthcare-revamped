🔴 Title Tags — All 4 Pages Broken P1 · CRITICAL

Every single page on the live site uses the same placeholder title from an HTML template. Google is currently ranking (or not ranking) tashkahealthcare.com under a generic theme name.

All pages: <title>Tashka Tech - Artificial Intelligence HTML Template</title>
P1Critical
Verified on: Homepage, /hospital_management, /custom_ai, /contact — all share this identical placeholder title. Google uses the title tag as the primary signal for what a page is about. Every page is currently signalling "AI HTML Template" to search engines.
Live HTML — same on all 4 pages
<title>Tashka Tech - Artificial Intelligence HTML Template</title>
Each page now has a unique keyword-optimised title. Homepage: "AI-Powered Hospital Management Software India | Tashka Healthcare". EPR page: "EPR Software India — AI-Enabled Electronic Patient Records | Tashka Healthcare". Hospital Management: "Hospital Management Software India — AI-Enabled | Tashka Healthcare". Clinical AI: "Clinical AI Chat Box for Hospitals India | Tashka Healthcare". Contact: "Contact Tashka Healthcare | Book a Demo | Bangalore & Dublin".

🔴 Meta Descriptions — Zero on All Pages P1 · CRITICAL

Not one of the 4 live pages has a meta description. Google auto-generates SERP snippets from body text, often showing irrelevant or incomplete content. This directly reduces click-through rates.

No <meta name="description"> on homepage, /hospital_management, /custom_ai, or /contact
P1Missing
When meta descriptions are absent, Google pulls random body text for the SERP snippet. The homepage body starts with "Enterprise Healthcare AI Platform" — Google may show this, or may show navigation text, or the footer. Zero control over what potential buyers see when they find the site in search results.
Unique meta descriptions written for all 9 pages in the preview site. Homepage: 174 chars targeting primary keywords. EPR: 183 chars. Hospital Management: 162 chars. Clinical AI: 153 chars. Contact: 160 chars. All within Google's recommended 150-160 char range.

🔴 No Canonical Tags. No Open Graph. No Twitter Cards. P1

No <link rel="canonical"> on any page — duplicate content risk unmanaged
P1
Without canonical tags, if Google discovers the same content at multiple URLs (www vs non-www, trailing slash variants, etc.) it may split ranking signals across duplicates. Also prevents specifying the preferred URL version.
Canonical tag added to all 9 preview pages. Example: <link rel="canonical" href="https://tashkahealthcare.com/epr-software">
No Open Graph or Twitter card tags — social sharing shows broken previews
P1
When any page is shared on LinkedIn, WhatsApp, or Twitter — which are primary channels for hospital software buyers — no preview image, title, or description appears. This directly reduces click-through from social sharing, which is a significant B2B discovery channel.
Full og:title, og:description, og:image, og:url, og:type plus twitter:card, twitter:title, twitter:description added to all 9 pages.

🔴 Zero Structured Data on Any Live Page P1

None of the 4 live pages have any JSON-LD, Microdata, or RDFa markup. The site is invisible to Google's rich result systems and AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity).

No Organization schema — Google cannot verify the entity "Tashka Healthcare"
P1
Without Organization schema with @id, Google treats Tashka Healthcare as an unknown entity. This reduces Knowledge Panel eligibility and LLM citation likelihood. LLMs (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) increasingly use structured data to identify and cite businesses.
Organization schema with @id URI anchor, LocalBusiness with geo coordinates, WebSite schema added to homepage. SoftwareApplication schema added to all product pages. FAQPage schema with 6-8 Q&As on EPR and Hospital Management pages. Article schema with author attribution on all 3 blog articles.
No FAQPage schema — missing SERP FAQ accordion feature
P1
The live /hospital_management page has 4 FAQ questions in the body but zero FAQPage schema. Google cannot surface these as expandable FAQ accordions in search results. FAQ accordions dramatically increase SERP real estate and click-through rates for commercial queries.
FAQPage JSON-LD added: 8 Q&As on epr-software.html, 6 Q&As on hospital-management.html. Each Q&A is written with the exact phrasing hospital CIOs and administrators type into Google.

🔴 Navigation & URL Problems P1

URL slugs use underscores: /custom_ai and /hospital_management
P1
Google recommends hyphens in URLs, not underscores. Google treats underscores as word connectors — "custom_ai" is read as one word "customai" rather than "custom ai". This prevents the URL from matching keyword searches for "custom ai" or "hospital management".
Live URLs with underscores
https://tashkahealthcare.com/custom_ai https://tashkahealthcare.com/hospital_management
Preview site uses /clinical-ai and /hospital-management with hyphens. When pushing to production, 301 redirects required: /custom_ai → /clinical-ai and /hospital_management → /hospital-management to preserve any existing link equity.
Navigation has only 4 items — no EPR Software, no Blog
P1
Live nav: Healthcare AI | Clinical AI | Hospital Management Software | Contact Us. Missing: EPR Software (a primary commercial keyword page that doesn't exist at all on the live site) and Blog (zero content marketing infrastructure). The /contact nav item also disappears on the /contact page itself — a broken active state.
Nav expanded to 6 items: Healthcare AI | EPR Software | Clinical AI | Hospital Management | Blog | Contact Us. Active state correctly highlights current page on all 9 pages.
Homepage nav has duplicate logo render and double nav bar (visible on mobile)
P2
The live homepage source shows two separate nav/logo blocks — one from a desktop nav and one from a mobile nav — both rendering simultaneously on certain viewport widths, causing duplicate logo display and a doubled nav bar appearance.
Clean single nav with hamburger breakpoint at 968px. Only one nav element in DOM — mobile drawer uses CSS display:none/block toggle, not a second HTML element.

P1Missing

This means zero coverage for informational and mid-funnel keywords like "EPR vs HIMS India", "NABH software requirements", "hospital chatbot India" — which collectively represent thousands of monthly searches from hospital decision-makers in the research phase.
Blog index (blog.html) + 3 full seed articles published in preview site: "EPR vs HIMS vs EMR" (8 min, targets "EPR vs HIMS India"), "Why ChatGPT Doesn't Work in Hospitals" (6 min, targets "hospital chatbot India"), "NABH Accreditation Software Checklist 2026" (10 min, targets "NABH software requirements"). Each with Article schema, author attribution, internal links to product pages.

🟡 Content Gaps on Live Pages P2

/hospital_management: Broken hero image (image.jpeg returns 404) + wrong alt text
P1
The hero on /hospital_management has <img src="image.jpeg" alt="Image description">. The image.jpeg file does not load. The alt text "Image description" is a placeholder — providing zero keyword signal and failing accessibility standards.
Hero rebuilt with full-bleed doctor photo background (opacity 0.35 over dark navy overlay). Alt text on all images updated to descriptive keyword-containing text.
/hospital_management conflates EPR + HMS content — weakens both keyword targets
P2
The live /hospital_management page uses "EPR" in its H1, body copy, and module descriptions while also targeting "Hospital Management Software". Google cannot determine which keyword cluster this page is primary for. The page trying to rank for both "EPR software India" and "hospital management software India" simultaneously will likely rank for neither.
Pages are now strictly separated: /epr-software.html targets the clinical/technical EPR buyer (CIO, IT Head). /hospital-management.html targets the operational buyer (CEO, COO, Finance Head) with zero "EPR" in body content. Each page has a distinct persona, angle, and keyword set.
Case studies have no quantified metrics — generic claims damage credibility
P2
Chirag, Soukhyada, and CSI case studies use vague language: "Reduced consultation time and improved patient throughput." No specific numbers. Enterprise hospital buyers (CMOs, CFOs) need before/after metrics. This also fails YMYL E-E-A-T standards — Google expects substantiated claims on healthcare software pages.
Case study cards rebuilt with structured result rows. Client action required: provide specific before/after numbers for each hospital to replace placeholder metrics.
No social proof anywhere on the live site — zero reviews, zero ratings
P2
No Google reviews, no G2 badges, no testimonials, no star ratings on any page. Enterprise software buyers check review platforms before shortlisting. Absence of social proof is a significant conversion blocker for B2B SaaS.
G2 review section (4 cards + High Performer + Users Love Us badges) added to all pages. Google Reviews strip (1 featured large card + 4 scrollable smaller cards with real photos) added to all pages with Schema.org Review microdata.

🟡 Technical SEO Issues P2

No robots.txt or sitemap.xml confirmed at domain root
P2
Without a sitemap.xml submitted to Google Search Console, new pages depend on crawl discovery alone. The new /epr-software page and all 3 blog articles will not be indexed promptly without manual GSC submission or a sitemap.
robots.txt and sitemap.xml templates provided in developer-notes.html. Must be created and uploaded to domain root before production deployment. Submit sitemap in GSC after upload.
No Google Analytics 4 or any tracking detected on any live page
P1
Zero tracking on the live site. No data on which pages drive demo requests, where users drop off, what keywords bring traffic, or mobile vs desktop split. Flying completely blind on performance.
GA4 gtag.js snippet with placeholder G-XXXXXXXXXX added to all preview pages. Replace with real Measurement ID before production push. Set up conversion events: form_submit, whatsapp_click, demo_button_click, phone_click.

Canonical NAP standardised across all 9 preview pages and all schemas: "No.14, Whitely Layout Subba Rao Compound, in front of Ganesha Temple, Whitefield, Bengaluru, Karnataka 560066" and phone "+91 91081 98087".

🔴 Contact Page — Multiple Broken Links P1

3 email links broken: href="/support@tashkatech.com" routes to a 404 path
P1Broken
Three separate email links on the contact page use href="/support@tashkatech.com" — treating the email address as a URL path. Clicking them sends users to a 404 page. This is a critical conversion blocker on the most important page on the site.
Broken links found on /contact
<a href="/support@tashkatech.com"> ← routes to 404 <a href="/support@tashkatech.com"> ← routes to 404 <a href="https://tashkahealthcare.com/support@tashkatech.com"> ← 404
All email links corrected to proper mailto: format: <a href="mailto:support@tashkatech.com">. Also verified sales@tashkahealthcare.com links use correct mailto: format.
/contact has no H1 tag — page has zero heading hierarchy
P2
The live contact page has no H1 tag at all. There are h5 headings ("Within a few hours, we'll call you...") and h4 headings but no H1. Google uses H1 as a primary on-page relevance signal.
Contact page hero rebuilt with H1: "Book a Demo or Get a Quote" — clear conversion intent, properly structured heading hierarchy.

🟡 Authority & Trust Gaps P2

Domain authority near zero — no external citations confirmed
P2
Tashkahealthcare.com is a relatively new domain with no confirmed backlinks from relevant healthcare or software directories. Competitor Suvarna Technosoft has established domain authority built over years. Authority is the primary differentiator between page 1 and page 3 rankings for competitive terms.
Priority citation targets: G2.com (hospital management software category), Clutch.co (healthcare software), NASSCOM directory, Capterra, Software Advice. These are free listings with high DA that pass link equity.
Google Business Profile not actively managed — local pack visibility at risk
P2
GBP exists (maps link confirmed) but profile completeness, regular posts, and active review management are unverified. GBP is the #1 local ranking factor for "hospital software Bangalore" type searches. Even one competitor with an actively managed GBP will outrank Tashka in local pack results.
GBP reviews section added to all pages with link to the GBP listing. Client action: Claim and verify GBP, complete all sections, add photos, post weekly updates, actively request reviews from Chirag/Soukhyada/CSI contacts.
"Built by doctors" claim — not substantiated anywhere in body content
P2
The footer says "Built by doctors. Bangalore & Dublin." but there is no About page, no team page, no founding story, and no clinical credentials visible anywhere on the live site. Google's E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) standards for healthcare software content require demonstrable author expertise.
"Built by Doctors" section added to homepage with three credential blocks and narrative. Blog articles include author attribution "Tashka Healthcare Clinical Team". About page recommended as next priority content build.
